## 4. Key Findings and Framework

### The SECI Model

**Four Modes of Knowledge Conversion:**

| Mode | From | To | Mechanism | Example |
|------|------|-----|-----------|---------|
| **Socialisation** | Tacit | Tacit | Shared experience, observation, imitation | Apprenticeship, on-the-job training |
| **Externalisation** | Tacit | Explicit | Articulation through metaphor, analogy, models | Design documentation, concept creation |
| **Combination** | Explicit | Explicit | Systematising, categorising, reconfiguring | Database integration, report compilation |
| **Internalisation** | Explicit | Tacit | Learning by doing, embodying explicit knowledge | Practicing documented procedures |

### Knowledge Spiral

Knowledge creation is a continuous spiral moving through:
1. Individual → Group → Organisation → Inter-organisation
2. Tacit ↔ Explicit conversion at each level
3. Amplification of knowledge through organisational levels

### Enabling Conditions

Nonaka identifies five conditions enabling organisational knowledge creation:
1. **Intention** - Organisational aspiration to goals
2. **Autonomy** - Individual freedom to act
3. **Fluctuation and Creative Chaos** - Stimulating interaction with environment
4. **Redundancy** - Intentional overlap of information
5. **Requisite Variety** - Matching internal diversity to environmental complexity

## 5. Framework Integration

### Connection to Theory-Building (Naur)

Nonaka's framework complements Naur's theory-building principle:
- Naur focuses on individual theory as tacit knowledge
- Nonaka addresses how tacit knowledge moves between individuals and becomes organisational
- Externalisation maps to attempts to document theory (with acknowledged limitations)
- Socialisation maps to how theory transfers between developers

### Connection to Transactive Memory (Wegner)

- TMS coordinates access to distributed knowledge
- SECI explains how that knowledge transforms between forms
- Combination mode relates to how explicit knowledge is integrated across TMS
- Socialisation mode explains tacit transfer that TMS cannot fully coordinate

### Implications for AI-Augmented Development

| SECI Mode | AI Capability | Human Role |
|-----------|---------------|------------|
| **Socialisation** | Cannot participate (requires shared experience) | Essential—mentoring, pairing |
| **Externalisation** | Can assist documentation | Provides tacit understanding to articulate |
| **Combination** | Strong capability (information processing) | Provides integration judgement |
| **Internalisation** | Cannot learn through practice | Essential—embodying knowledge |

## 6. Limitations and Validation Status

### Known Critiques

**Gourlay (2006) critique:**
- Questions whether tacit knowledge can be "converted" to explicit
- Argues Nonaka misinterprets Polanyi
- Suggests Socialisation mechanism is underspecified

**Empirical limitations:**
- Original evidence primarily from Japanese context
- Western replications show inconsistent results
- Difficult to operationalise and measure SECI modes

### Validation Status

- ✓ **Conceptual framework**: Widely adopted and influential
- ✓ **Tacit/explicit distinction**: Well-grounded in Polanyi
- ⚠ **SECI modes as conversion**: Theoretically contested
- ⚠ **Socialisation mechanism**: Underspecified
- ⚠ **Cross-cultural validity**: Japanese context may not generalise
- ⚠ **Empirical measurement**: Difficult to operationalise
